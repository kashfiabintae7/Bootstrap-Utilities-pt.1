Billie Eilish

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Billie Eilish Pirate Baird O'Connell (/ˈaɪlɪʃ/ EYE-lish;[2] born December 18, 2001) is an American singer-songwriter and musician. She first gained public attention in 2015 with her debut single "Ocean Eyes", written and produced by her brother Finneas O'Connell, with whom she collaborates on music and live shows. In 2017, she released her debut extended play (EP), a Don't Smile at Me. Commercially successful, it reached the top 15 of record charts in numerous countries, including the US, UK, Canada, and Australia.

Eilish's debut studio album, When We All Fall Asleep, Where Do We Go? (2019), debuted atop the US Billboard 200 and UK Albums Chart and was one of the year's best-selling albums. Its single "Bad Guy" became the first by an artist born in the 21st century to top the US Billboard Hot 100 and be certified Diamond by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA). The next year, Eilish performed the theme song "No Time to Die" for the James Bond film No Time to Die, which topped the UK Singles Chart and won the Academy Award for Best Original Song in 2022. Her subsequent singles "Everything I Wanted", "My Future", "Therefore I Am", and "Your Power", each peaked within the top ten in the US and UK.

Eilish's second studio album, Happier Than Ever (2021), topped the charts in 25 countries. She wrote and performed "What Was I Made For?" for the fantasy film Barbie which became her second number-one single in the UK and earned her a second Academy Award. Her third album, Hit Me Hard and Soft (2024), was met with critical acclaim and spawned the US top-five singles "Lunch" and "Birds of a Feather", with the latter becoming her first number-one on the Billboard Global 200.

Eilish has received many accolades, including two Academy Awards, nine Grammy Awards, two American Music Awards, twenty Guinness World Records, seven MTV Video Music Awards, four Brit Awards, and two Golden Globe Awards. She is the second artist in Grammy history to win all four general field categories—Record of the Year, Album of the Year, Song of the Year, as well as Best New Artist— in the same year. Eilish is also the first person born in the 21st century to win an Academy Award and the youngest ever two-time winner. [3] She was featured on Time magazine's inaugural Time 100 Next list in 2019 and the Time 100 in 2021. [4] According to the RIAA and Billboard, Eilish is the 26th-highest-certified digital singles artist [5] and one of the most successful artists of the 2010s. [6] She was honored as one of the BBC 100 Women in December 2022. [7] Eilish has a history of political activism, focusing on climate change awareness, women's reproductive rights, and gender equality.

Early life

Billie Eilish Pirate Baird O'Connell was born in Los Angeles, California, on December 18, 2001. [8] She is the daughter of actress and teacher Maggie Baird[9][10] and actor Patrick O'Connell, [11] both of whom are also musicians and work on Eilish's tours. [12][13][14] Eilish is of Irish and Scottish descent. [15] She was conceived via in vitro fertilization. [16] Her middle name, Eilish, was originally meant to be her first name, while Pirate was to have been her middle name. But after her grandfather William Baird died while her mother was pregnant, her parents decided to name her Billie, after her grandfather. [17] She grew up in Los Angeles's Highland Park neighborhood. [18][19]

Eilish and her older brother Finneas were homeschooled by Baird, a decision their parents made to spend time with them and give them the freedom to pursue their interests. [11][14]Baird taught Eilish and Finneas the basics of songwriting. Eilish said her brother and mother inspired her to get into music. [21] Their parents encouraged the siblings to express themselves and explore whatever they wanted, including art, dancing, and acting.

Eilish started playing the ukulele at age six. She performed at talent shows and joined the Los Angeles Children's Chorus at age eight. [22][20][23] She wrote her first "real" song at age 11 for her mother's songwriting class. The song is about the zombie apocalypse, inspired by the television series The Walking Dead, from which she took script lines and episode titles. [24][21] Eilish took some acting auditions, which she disliked, but she enjoyed recording background dialogue for crowd scenes and worked on the films Diary of a Wimpy Kid, Ramona and Beezus, and the X-Men series.[20] Eilish also took dance classes until 2016, when a growth plate injury ended her dance career and she focused on recording music. [25][26][22]

Career

2015–2017: Don't Smile at Me

In 2015, 13-year-old Eilish began recording music with her brother Finneas, releasing their first songs, "She's Broken" and "Fingers Crossed," on SoundCloud. On November 18, 2015, Eilish released "Ocean Eyes," which was written, produced, and mixed by Finneas. The song gained attention and led to her signing with Apple Music's Platoon in January 2016. [26][22][13]

In June 2016, Eilish released "Six Feet Under" on SoundCloud, followed by a homemade music video. [42]Later that year, she signed with Darkroom and Interscope Records. [31][43] Both "Ocean Eyes" and "Six Feet Under" were re-released as singles in November 2016. [44][45] Eilish continued to release singles in 2017, including "Bellyache" in February[49] and "Bored" in March, which was featured in the 13 Reasons Why soundtrack.

On August 11, 2017, Eilish released her debut EP Don't Smile at Me, which reached number 14 on the US Billboard 200. [59] Eilish’s success grew with Spotify promotion and her recognition as Apple Music’s Up Next artist in September 2017. She also collaborated with Vince Staples on the track "&Burn" in December 2017. [35][36]37]

2018–2020: When We All Fall Asleep, Where Do We Go?

In February 2018, Eilish went on her second tour, the Where's My Mind Tour, concluding in April 2018. >"Bitches Broken Hearts" was re-released in March. [72] She released "Party Favor" [73] on vinyl and collaborated with Khalid on "Lovely" for 13 Reasons Why season 2. In July, she released "You Should See Me in a Crown" [75] and performed at Mo Pop Festival. Eilish was featured in Vanity Fair’s "73 Questions" in October 2018. [78] She signed with Next Management for fashion and was named to Forbes 30 Under 30. She also released "Come Out and Play" for an Apple Inc. commercial.

In early 2019, Don't Smile at Me hit 1 billion streams on Spotify. She released "Bury a Friend" and "When I Was Older" [85], inspired by Roma. Eilish partnered with YouTube for a documentary. [78]

Her album When We All Fall Asleep, Where Do We Go? debuted at number one on the Billboard 200 and UK charts in March 2019, with "Bad Guy" topping charts in August 2019. [92] She began her When We All Fall Asleep Tour at Coachella and partnered with Apple Music for Music Lab. Her Where Do We Go? World Tour [101] was cut short by COVID-19. In November 2019, Eilish released "Everything I Wanted" and was nominated for six Grammy Awards, winning Record of the Year, Song of the Year, Album of the Year, and Best New Artist. [105][106] >

Eilish also wrote and performed the No Time to Die Bond theme, becoming the youngest artist to do so. At the 62nd Grammy Awards, she won four major categories. [110][111] > During the pandemic, she performed at virtual concerts.

In April 2020, "Ilomilo" was sent to Italian radio. [110][111] In July, Eilish released "My Future" . She became the youngest to appear on the Forbes Celebrity 100 list, earning $53 million. [110][111] In October 2020, Eilish announced Where Do We Go? The Livestream, supporting event crew members during the pandemic. She released "Therefore I Am" [110][111] in November. She performed at the Jingle Ball in December 2020.

2021–2023: Happier Than Ever and Guitar Songs

In January 2021, Eilish released the single "Lo Vas a Olvidar", featuring Rosalía [122], part of the Euphoria soundtrack. The documentary Billie Eilish: The World's a Little Blurry premiered on Apple TV+, praised for its in-depth look at Eilish’s rise to fame. At the 63rd Annual Grammy Awards, she won two awards: Best Song Written for Visual Media for her Bond theme and Record of the Year for "Everything I Wanted" [124]. Eilish published a book of photographs, By - Billie Eilish. [125]

On April 27, 2021, Eilish announced the release of her second album, Happier Than Ever, on July 30, preceded by singles like "My Future" , "Therefore I Am" , and "Your Power" [130]. The album was followed by a special vinyl edition and a concert film released with Disney+. [131][132][133]

In 2021, Eilish faced backlash for past actions, including mouthing an anti-Asian slur and was accused of queerbaiting for her caption on "Lost Cause" [140]. She apologized, explaining she was young and unaware of the slur’s meaning. [143][144] In 2022, Eilish won an Academy Award for Best Original Song for "No Time to Die" and became the youngest headliner at Glastonbury and Coachella. [148][149] She also contributed three songs to the Turning Red soundtrack. [150]

In 2022, Eilish released the EP Guitar Songs with tracks like "TV" and "The 30th" [152]. Eilish worked with Apple Music to showcase a concert film from her Happier Than Ever tour. Eilish debuted in the series Swarm in 2023. She wrote the song "What Was I Made For?" [163] for the Barbie movie, winning an Academy Award for Best Original Song and two Grammys in 2024. [164]

2024–present: Hit Me Hard and Soft

On April 8, 2024, Eilish announced her third album, Hit Me Hard and Soft, released on May 17, 2024. She also announced the Hit Me Hard and Soft: The Tour for September 2024–July 2025. Eilish was featured in Fortnite Festival and appeared in London with Amelia Dimoldenberg on Chicken Shop Date. She also guest-hosted CBeebies' Bedtime Story. [100]

In August, she remixed Charli XCX's "Guess," donating proceeds to charity. On August 11, 2024, Eilish performed "Birds of a Feather" during the 2024 Summer Olympics closing ceremony.

Artistry

Musical style, songwriting, and music videos

Eilish has a soprano vocal range. Critics describe her voice as "ethereal" and "whispery," with a "husky, slurring" quality. Her music blends genres like pop, electropop, goth-pop, and emo pop. Her songs often explore themes of depression.

Eilish and her brother Finneas collaborate on songwriting, with Finneas producing and performing her music. They aim to write conversational and deeper lyrics. Eilish directed her first music video for "Xanny" in 2019 after wanting to do so since age 14. [102][103]

Public image and recognitions

Eilish has won numerous awards, including nine Grammy Awards, two American Music Awards, two Academy Awards, and nineteen Guinness World Records. She is the youngest, and first female, artist to win the four main Grammy categories in the same year. [200]

In 2022, she won the Academy Award for Best Original Song for "No Time to Die" and became the first person born in the 21st century to win an Oscar. In 2024, she won her second Academy Award for "What Was I Made For?" from Barbie, making her the youngest person to win two Academy Awards and joining a select group of songs that won both an Oscar and a Grammy. [210][211][212]

Style

Eilish's fashion style, centered around oversized, baggy clothing, has attracted significant media attention. She has stated that dressing this way makes her feel noticed and aims to stand out from others. In 2019, she explained that her style helped her "prove to people that [she's] more important than they think" and enjoys being "kind of intimidating" to capture attention. In 2019, she said: "Over time it's kind of become a thing, 'Billie Eilish, the creepy, weird, scary girl.' And I don't like that. It's lame. I just don't want to stay one thing. [239][240]

She also revealed she dresses in baggy clothes to avoid body judgment. In May 2020, Eilish released "Not My Responsibility," a short film addressing body shaming. [245] Eilish later appeared on the cover of British Vogue in 2021 in a corset-based photoshoot, and at the 2021 Met Gala, she wore an Oscar de la Renta gown, with the condition that the brand stop using real fur. [249][251]

Products and endorsements

In April 2019, Eilish released a clothing collection with Takashi Murakami, inspired by her "You Should See Me in a Crown" music video. She also collaborated with Adobe Creative Cloud for a social media art contest. [253][254] Later that month, she appeared in Calvin Klein's #MyCalvins ad campaign and participated in the Ad Council's mental health awareness campaign. [259][261]

Eilish fronted MCM Worldwide's fall 2019 campaign and collaborated with Freak City on a clothing line. [263][264] In August 2019, she partnered with Apple for Music Lab sessions, allowing customers to experiment with her song. [269]

Eilish's collaboration with Siberia Hills sparked controversy due to plagiarized designs used in her clothing line, though it was clarified she had no involvement in the plagiarism. [270] In November 2021, Eilish launched her vegan and cruelty-free perfume brand, "Eilish." In October 2024, she teamed up with Converse for a limited-edition Chuck Taylor collection, allowing customization with lyrics from her songs. [277]

Advocacy

Eilish is an active political voice, focusing on climate change, women's rights, and social justice. She signed an open letter urging action against climate-induced poverty and hosted a climate seminar, Overheated, discussing sustainable fashion and youth activism. [279][282] Her 2019 single All the Good Girls Go to Hell addresses climate change, and she supported CoralWatch’s campaign on the Great Barrier Reef. Eilish advocates for animal rights, veganism, and has criticized industries like airy, mink fur, and wool. [288][289]

Eilish’s work on women’s rights includes her 2020 short film Not My Responsibility, which addresses body shaming and societal standards. Her song "Your Power" critiques the sexual exploitation of young women. [298][299] She is a vocal supporter of abortion rights, particularly after Texas's 2021 anti-abortion laws and the overturning of Roe v. Wade, which she condemned during her 2022 Glastonbury performance. [300][302]

In 2020, she encouraged voting in the US presidential election and endorsed Joe Biden. In 2023, Eilish helped fund REVERB's Music Decarbonization Project. [307] At the 2024 Academy Awards, she wore a pin calling for a ceasefire in the Israel-Hamas war. In 2024, she and Finneas endorsed the Harris-Walz ticket for the US presidential election. [310]

Personal Life

Eilish lived with her parents in Los Angeles until 2019. She was diagnosed with Tourette syndrome at 11, has synesthesia, and has struggled with depression. Eilish has also spoken about experiencing sexual abuse as a child. [315][316]

Eilish is queer and has had public relationships with rapper Brandon Adams (2018–2019), actor Matthew Tyler Vorce (2021–2022), and singer Jesse Rutherford (2022–2023). In 2024, she stated that she no longer wishes to discuss her dating life. [319][320]

In a 2020 podcast, Eilish shared that she was once religious but became "almost anti-religious" as she grew older. She identifies as neutral in terms of religion, open to all beliefs but without a definitive stance on any. [326][327]
